DOWNLOAD PARTICIPANT IMAGES

<do every time after getting devices back from a new research participant>

1. DOWNLOAD IMAGES FROM CAMERA (Using DohertyBrowser software)

    1. Open the software (as above)
    1. Click the dropdown box in the middle of the opening screen to select a user
    2. a. If you select “<new participant>”, then enter a ParticipantID in the new text box that appears
    3. b. [Click here to clean up the list of participants shown in the list box||manipulateBrowserData]
    1. Click the “START” button that appears
    1. Plug the camera into your computer via the USB cable
    1. Click the “Add” button
    1. Click the “Start” button (indicates software has identified plugged in camera)

      <it now takes a couple of minutes for the data to download>

    1. The main screen then shows the middle image from the “chunk” of images captured each time the camera was switched on in a given day (if there are images)
    1. You can click on any “chunk” image to then show all the images captured within it
    2. a. To delete any image, press “toggle view”, move forwards/backwards to the intended image, press the “Delete” (bottom left corner of screen), then click “Close” (top right) to confirm the deletion of images from this event/chunk/grouping/episode
    1. You can click the “Calendar” button to select another day of images
    1. When finished, do not plug the camera out, unto the software allows you to click the application “Quit” button
    1. The images will now be deleted from the camera.

2. IMPORT IMAGES ALREADY DOWNLOADED TO PC BY DohertyBrowser (Using DohertyBrowser software)

<if you want to do a new type of analysis on a participant's data, it is generally helpful to create a new participantID account, and then reload the participant's data>

    1. Open the software (as above), and navigate into the main screen
    1. Click the “Add” button
    1. Click “Advanced”
    1. Click “Download from PC…”
    1. Click “Browse” beside “Path of images already on your PC:”
    1. Navigate to the folder where the images are located
    1. Then click “Start”

3. IMPORT IMAGES MANUALLY COPIED FROM CAMERA TO DISK (Using DohertyBrowser software)

<For cases where images were manually copied from the camera, rather than automatically downloaded using the DohertyBrowser. This will read the images from a specified location that is treated as if it were the camera plugged into your computer, and copy the images to the “PC Destination:” location. The images can then be used for normal analysis in the browser.>

    1. Open the software (as above), and navigate into the main screen
    1. Click the “Add” button
    1. Click “Browse” beside “SenseCam or Vicon Revue path:”
    1. Navigate to the location on disk where you manually copied the participant’s images to, and click on the “DATA” folder
    1. Then click “Start”

Note that the image directory structure should be the same as on the Vicon Autographer device. i.e.
DATA / <dateFolderContainingImages> & image_table.txt

  • Optionally, you can choose where to store the re-structured image directory too. After step 6, and before step 7, click "Browse" beside "PC Destination:", and then navigate to the desired location.
  • If you wish, you can set the browser to not delete the images from your source file structure/directory. Open the “C:\AidenDoherty\DohertyBrowser\SenseCamBrowser1.exe.config” file, and edit the <add key="autoDeleteImagesOnUpload" value="1"/>” line. The value 1 means the source images will be deleted, the value 0 means they will not be deleted.

4. IMPORT AND RELATE EXTERNAL DATA TO WEARABLE CAMERA IMAGES (Using DohertyBrowser software)

    1. Prepare your heartRate/GPS/accelerometer episodes into a CSV file the same format as:
    2. a. Navigate to https://sensecambrowser.codeplex.com/
    3. b. Click “Downloads” along the top menu bar
    4. c. Download “episodeDefsEg.csv”
    1. Open the software
    1. Create a new user, e.g. “P001Accelerometer”
    1. Navigate into the main screen
    1. Click the “Add” button
    1. Click “Advanced”
    1. Click “Download from PC” if downloading images from PC (generally this will be the case)
    1. Click “Browse” beside “Path of images already on your PC:”
    1. Navigate to the folder where the images are located
    1. Click “Browse” beside “Episode definitions:”
    1. Navigate to the .csv file you prepared in step 1
    1. Then click “Start”
